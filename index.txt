<section id="about">
  <h2>About Us</h2>
  <p>Blueprints for Tomorrow is a student-founded nonprofit dedicated to helping elementary and middle school students learn real-world engineering through project-based learning. Our mission is to provide a variety of students to engineering resources and a structured, mentor-guided environment to explore, design, and create innovative solutions to real problems.</p>
</section>

<section id="program">
  <h2>Our 8-Week Pilot Program</h2>
  <p>Students participate in an 8-week online engineering studio focused on solving real-world problems. Each week, students work on:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Week 1 – Problem Framing: Define the problem, stakeholders, constraints<li>
    <li>Week 2 – Brainstorming & Tradeoffs: Generate solutions, compare pros/cons<li>
    <li>Week 3 – System Design: Block diagrams, system logic, components<li><li>
    <li>Week 4 – Modeling & Simulation: Online tools, sketches, math modeling, pseudocode<li>
    <li>Week 5 – Failure & Risk Analysis: What could go wrong? How to mitigate?<li>
    <li>Week 6 – Iteration: Refine designs based on feedback<li>
    <li>Week 7 – Final Proposal: Written and visual engineering proposal<li>
    <li>Week 8 – Showcase & Reflection: Presentation + reflection on learning<li>
  </ul>
  <p>Example projects include designing assistive technology, low-cost environmental monitoring systems, or disaster preparedness tools. Students finish the program with a tangible engineering portfolio piece and new problem-solving skills.</p>
</section>
